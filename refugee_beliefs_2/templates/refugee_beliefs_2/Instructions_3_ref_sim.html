Both groups of citizens have to decide how much should be given to the refugee.

  • The exact decisions to be made is similar for both groups, and is explained below
  • Participants from group A and participants from group B are making their decisions at the same time (simultaneously).

Each host citizen has to make the following (private and independant) decision:

  • The amount that he can give to the refugee varies between 0% and 20% of his common good.
  • To do this, he needs to move a slider to the position you want. Example:
  • The slider varies between 0% and 20%, with 20% corresponding to one fifth of the common good of his group.
  • A citizen is chosen at random, whose decision determines how many points from the common good of his group are given to the refugee.
  • The remaining points are divided equally between the citizens.
    • Depending on how much is given to the refugee, this amount varies between 20% and 25% of the common good of his group.
