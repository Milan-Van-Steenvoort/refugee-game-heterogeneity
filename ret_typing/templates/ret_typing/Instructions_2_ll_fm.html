There are two types of players in the decision game

  • Citizens
    • Citizens are participants who take part in the typing task where you have to fill in 5 sequences of numbers and letters and earn 25 points (the task you just practiced). All participants who are assigned the role of citizen perform the exact same task.
    • 40% of the points that are earned in the typing task are withheld and collected in a common account. You can think of these as taxes that citizens have to pay.
      • 40% of 25 points: 10 points
    • The remaining 60% are a citizen's personal earnings.
      • 60% of 25 points: 15 points
  • Refugee
    • The refugee (a participant in another study) does not participate in the typing task and therefore cannot contribute to the common account.
    • The refugee has no own income.
  • The roles that are assigned in this decision task are fictional and do not necessarily correspond to reality.

Each game consists of one refugee and two groups of four host citizens

  • You have been assigned the role of a citizen.
  • Each group consists of 4 citizens. You are one of the 4 citizens in a group.
  • Each citizen from your group, including yourself, earns 25 points in the typing task.
  • Therefore, the total amount of points contributed to the common account of your own group is 40 points (4 x 10 points).
  • Each citizen from the other group earns 25 points in the typing task.
  • Therefore, the total amount of points contributed to the common account of the other group is 40 points (4 x 10 points).
